The Virtual Cowboy

Abstract:

Virtual Cowboy is a collaborative initiative to experiment a product concept and potential startup partnering with California Ag Solutions, an agronomy consulting company based out of Madera, supporting sustainable agricultural operations throughout California. Overgrazing is a constant problem for cattle operation: the bigger the herd, or the bigger the paddock, the more cowboys are needed to monitor and move the cows. Virtual fencing technologies exist, but there is no complete solution to manage herd operations from computers or phones.

Solution:

Create an application that demonstrates and simulates how a remote herd management tool would function, with an intuitive user experience

Features:

Tablet-friendly web application with map interface | Creating an editing,deleting ranch/fence boundaries on the map | Map displays cow locations in real time | Database storage of boundaries and cow position in backend | Simulate cow behavior (wandering, reacting to boundaries) | Simulate hazing and rounding up cows | No-Go zones for obstacles (buildings, dangerous terrain)

Stack

Front-end: React.js | React-leaflet | leaflet-Geoman

Back-end: Flask | Python | Socket.io | Shapely | SQLALchemy

Database: SQLite

Aquatic Drone Mapper

I built an aquatic drone to operate remotely using a skid steering thruster system to collect sonar sensor and gps coordinate data for 3D map making of lake beds. Attached a camera that streams and records footage, and was operated remotely too. I used a Cube Orange+ for the Flight Controller and MissionPlanner for adding firmware

Some Hardware and Sensors

Battery on the left for powering up the small electronics on the drone. Such as the herelink reciever to the right of it, the Cube Orange+ to the far right, and the circular disc is a Here3 GPS unit

More Hardware

I connected two 12V batteries in series to create a 24V cell connected to a voltage regulator mounted on the wall of the box. From There 2 ESC that connect to the two servo propellors that are connected through the two aluminum prongs.

Omnidirectional Thruster System

I built a remotely operated omnidirectional thruster system for a 20ft long rectangular boat. Along with 2 additional buttons to remotely activate a 5v relay that turns on actuators to raise and lower a platform of UV lights.

Main Hardware

Cube Orange+ for the Flight Controller connected to the herelink reciever on the left. 4 ESCs on each corner representative to the assigned motor. At the bottom is a Here3+ GPS Unit

After completing the calibration of all the electronics, integrating it into the actual boat surfaced more technical problems.
